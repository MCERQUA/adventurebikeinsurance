# Competitor Research — adventurebikeinsurance.com

**Project:** adventurebikeinsurance.com — specialist insurance for adventure / dual-sport motorcycle owners (ADV touring, dual-sport, off-road, accessory/farkle coverage, gear).
**Brand / Agency:** Contractors Choice Agency.
**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Method:** WebSearch (Brave) + WebFetch of competitor pages, spaced to respect rate limits. Market: primarily US, with one US-specialty broker and one Canadian specialist for contrast.

The market splits into three tiers:
1. **National direct carriers** — Progressive, Dairyland, GEICO, Markel, Rider Insurance. Huge brands, broad coverage, generic motorcycle messaging. They serve ADV bikes but do not specialize in them.
2. **Specialty / niche brokers** — insuredmymotorcycle.com, Riders Plus (Canada). Smaller, more rider-focused, but thin on hard policy detail.
3. **Content / comparison sites** — BikeBound. Educational; refer riders out to carriers. Useful for understanding the SEO content landscape, not direct competitors for the quote.

## Per-Competitor Analysis

### 1. Progressive — progressive.com/motorcycle
**Tier:** National direct carrier (the dominant player).
**Homepage structure:** Conversion-focused. Header nav (products/claims/resources/company) → hero with inline quote form (ZIP + state) → value props → detailed coverage explanations → FAQ → cross-sell products → footer.
**Coverages/services:** Liability (required); comprehensive & collision (recommended); OEM parts replacement (included); optional total-loss coverage for new bikes, guest passenger liability, safety apparel coverage, accessories/custom parts coverage. Insures cruisers, sport, touring, dirt bikes, vintage, custom, scooters, ATVs, UTVs, e-bikes, autocycles.
**Accessory/farkle detail:** $3,000 accessory & custom-parts coverage included automatically with comp+collision; buy up to **$30,000**; coverage **never depreciates** the custom parts. This is the strongest standard accessory offer in the market.
**Total loss:** Full MSRP of a brand-new bike (minus deductible) if totaled within model-year window (≤1 yr new policy, ≤2 yr renewal).
**Primary CTAs:** "Get a quote" (above fold), "Call 1-855-347-3939", "Continue previous quote", "Find an agent".
**Trust elements:** "1 of 3 motorcycle riders choose Progressive"; "50+ years handling motorcycle claims"; "#1 insurance website (Keynova Q4 2025)"; sponsors world's largest consumer motorcycle show tour; 24/7 assistance; from $75/yr liability.
**Discounts:** Pay-in-full, motorcycle endorsement, multi-policy bundling (~5% on auto), homeowner/renter/condo bundles.
**Content depth:** Deep. Large "Answers" knowledge base (totaled motorcycle, what coverage means, etc.).

### 2. Dairyland — dairylandinsurance.com/motorcycle
**Tier:** National direct carrier; positions as the custom/non-standard-bike specialist.
**Homepage structure:** Classic landing page — desert-motorcycle hero illustration → insurance overview → coverage detail → motorcycle types → quote process → FAQ → resources.
**Coverages/services:** Bodily injury & property damage liability, guest passenger liability, collision, comprehensive; optional/special equipment coverage, replacement cost, roadside assistance, rental reimbursement. Covers cruisers, touring, sport, choppers, three-wheelers, dirt bikes, mopeds, scooters, electric.
**Standout:** Exclusive **OEM replacement coverage** for custom/modified bikes (claims no other top-five carrier includes this). Special-equipment coverage requires you to specify limits upfront.
**Primary CTAs:** "Get a Quote" (multiple), "Find an agent", "Continue a quote", phone 866-324-7952.
**Trust elements:** "Since 1966, millions of riders"; underwritten by Sentry Insurance Group; reviews section literally headlined **"Adventure-seekers love Dairyland."**
**Discounts:** Multi-cycle, H.O.G. member, homeownership, rider-training, higher-deductible, pay-in-full. From "$15/month."
**Content depth:** Moderate; resource articles (e.g., motorcycle customization tips).

### 3. GEICO — geico.com/motorcycle-insurance
**Tier:** National direct carrier; price-led ("cheap cycle insurance" landing pages).
**Coverages/services:** Standard liability/comp/collision; accessory coverage — first **$2,000** of accessories & modifications included with comp/collision (state-dependent; saddlebags, backrests, seats, chrome, CB radios); some policies include safety riding apparel. Optional roadside assistance in US/Canada with specialized flatbed towing.
**Primary CTAs:** "Get a free quote" / "Save today."
**Trust elements:** **A++ (Superior) A.M. Best** financial strength rating; large national brand recognition. (BBB shows A- with notable negative review volume — a reputational soft spot.)
**Discounts:** Up to 10% each for transfer, multi-bike, mature rider, renewal; MSF safety-course discount.
**Weakness for ADV:** Lowest standard accessory limit ($2,000) of the majors; generic messaging, no off-road/adventure positioning.

### 4. Markel — markel.com (Markel Specialty motorcycle)
**Tier:** National carrier, specialty insurer positioning ("tailor-made coverage").
**Coverages/services:** Includes **$1,000** for riding gear, custom chrome, windshield, fairings and other add-ons; buy additional in $1,000 increments up to **$30,000**. Markets "coverages that go beyond the basics" and right-sizing so you don't overpay.
**Standout:** Explicitly bundles **riding gear** into accessory coverage — one of the few to name gear directly. Long-standing specialty-insurer brand with strong financial backing.
**Content depth:** Resource library on motorcycle accessory coverage and selection guides.
**Weakness for ADV:** Generic motorcycle framing; no dedicated adventure/dual-sport landing experience.

### 5. Rider Insurance — rider.com (Plymouth Rock / Prudential alliance)
**Tier:** Regional specialist (NJ/PA-focused), motorcycle-exclusive heritage.
**Coverages/services:** "Rider Assurance Packages" bundled free into every policy (Essential Assurance + Assurance Plus benefits). **GAP coverage** (difference between loan balance and ACV on total loss). **Rider Accessory Package** up to **$8,000** standard, expandable to **$30,000**. First-Party Med-Pay for rider and passenger.
**Trust elements:** "For over 40 years, focused exclusively on motorcycle insurance"; one of the largest motorcycle insurers in NJ/PA; alliance with Plymouth Rock Assurance adds scale.
**Standout:** Strongest *standard* accessory limit ($8k) of any carrier reviewed, plus GAP and bundled assurance packages. Motorcycle-only heritage = credibility.
**Weakness:** Geographically limited (NJ/PA core); no adventure/off-road specialization.

### 6. insuredmymotorcycle.com — Dual-Sport & Adventure Bike specialty broker (Diamond Bar, CA)
**Tier:** US specialty broker — closest direct positioning to our concept.
**Coverages/services they message:** Off-road protection (explicitly warns "standard policies may not cover accidents off paved roads"); custom parts/accessories (panniers, crash bars, nav systems); breakdown/roadside after mechanical failure; trip coverage for gear and towing on extended adventures.
**Primary CTAs:** "Get a quote online or over the phone", prominent click-to-call (866-226-4436), "Call Now" in header.
**Trust elements:** Local business address; comparison framing vs Progressive/GEICO; MSF course discount mention. **Missing:** no years in business, no licensing, no ratings/reviews shown.
**Content depth:** Moderate — covers basics and ADV themes but **lacks granular detail** (limits, deductibles, exclusions, international policy specifics) that serious ADV riders want.
**Key takeaway:** They've claimed the ADV/dual-sport positioning and SEO angle but execute it thinly — beatable on depth and trust.

### 7. Riders Plus — ridersplus.com (Ontario, Canada)
**Tier:** Specialty broker, motorcycle-only heritage (international comparison point).
**Coverages/services:** Started as a motorcycle-only brokerage; now full personal lines (home, auto, boat, cottage). Rider-focused service; emphasizes understanding motorcyclists' needs.
**Trust elements:** "Insuring Ontario riders **since 1996**"; motorcycle-only origin story; broker for multiple carriers (Aviva, Echelon, etc.); toll-free 1-877-251-4504.
**Content depth:** Moderate; rider-centric brand voice. Public pages thin on agreed-value / adventure-touring specifics (broker model — detail lives in quotes).
**Key takeaway:** Demonstrates the "rider-by-riders specialist broker" brand model works and earns loyalty — but doesn't surface hard ADV coverage detail.

### 8. BikeBound — bikebound.com (Dual-Sport Insurance guide)
**Tier:** Content/comparison/community site (not a direct quote competitor).
**Coverages discussed:** Comprehensive & theft, collision, liability (BI/PD), roadside. Heavily emphasizes the **off-road exclusion problem** ("many policies have exclusions for off-road riding").
**CTAs:** Two ZIP-code quote-comparison widgets (lead-gen for carriers).
**Trust elements:** Minimal — no named carriers, licensing, or founding date.
**Content depth:** Moderate educational value (dual-sport definitions, popular models). Reads as general guidance, sends riders to agents.
**Key takeaway:** Confirms the #1 ADV rider pain point in the content landscape: **off-road riding exclusions** in standard policies. Big SEO/content opening.

## Common Patterns (what virtually every competitor has)

These are table-stakes — our site MUST include them:

1. **Hero with an inline quote form (ZIP/state) + a click-to-call phone number.** Universal across carriers and brokers. Quote and call are the two primary CTAs everywhere.
2. **Core coverage explainer block:** liability, comprehensive, collision — plus the optional layer (roadside, rental reimbursement, total loss/GAP).
3. **Accessory / custom-parts coverage callout.** Everyone offers it; differentiator is the *limit* and whether it depreciates. Standard limits range $1,000 (Markel) – $2,000 (GEICO) – $3,000 (Progressive) – $8,000 (Rider), all expandable to ~$30,000.
4. **Motorcycle-types coverage list** (cruiser/sport/touring/dirt/electric, etc.) so visitors self-identify.
5. **Trust bar:** years in business ("since 19XX" / "40+ years"), A.M. Best rating, customer/market-share stat, and an underwriter/licensing disclosure.
6. **Discounts module:** multi-bike, safety-course (MSF), pay-in-full, mature/experienced rider, bundling/transfer/renewal.
7. **FAQ section** + a **resource/blog knowledge base** (Progressive and Dairyland set the depth bar; brokers under-invest here).
8. **"Find an agent" / agent-assisted path** alongside self-serve quote (carriers), or a human-broker promise (specialty brokers).

## Gaps & Opportunities (how a specialist ADV site wins)

The majors are generalists; the existing ADV specialists (insuredmymotorcycle, BikeBound) are thin on depth and trust. The whitespace is **deep, credible, adventure-specific** content and coverage framing. Top opportunities:

1. **Own the off-road / dual-use exclusion problem.** Every competitor either ignores it or only flags it as a worry. Be the site that clearly explains where coverage ends on a fire road vs a closed off-road park, and offer/source coverage that follows the rider off pavement. This is the #1 ADV pain point (confirmed by BikeBound and insuredmymotorcycle). Make it the homepage hero hook.

2. **High-limit, non-depreciating farkle/accessory coverage as the headline.** ADV bikes routinely carry $5k–$15k in farkles (panniers, crash bars, skid plates, nav, aux lighting, suspension). Lead with a specific, generous accessory limit that **never depreciates** (beat/match Progressive's model) and itemize ADV farkles by name — competitors list "chrome and saddlebags," not skid plates and Touratech racks. Speak the rider's language.

3. **Riding-gear coverage, explicitly and generously.** Only Markel ($1k) and GEICO (sometimes) name gear; ADV gear (helmet, ADV suit, boots, armor) easily exceeds $2k. Offer a clear gear limit and call it out — most riders don't know it can be covered.

4. **International / cross-border touring coverage.** Completely absent across all US competitors (only mentioned in non-US/UK contexts). ADV riders ride Baja, Canada, Trans-America Trail, Pan-American. Offer or clearly explain coverage for US/Canada/Mexico touring, trip-interruption, and gear-while-traveling. This is a genuine unclaimed niche.

5. **Agreed-value / total-loss protection for modified & older ADV bikes.** Carriers cap total-loss at MSRP for new bikes only; a 2015 GS with $10k in farkles is underprotected by ACV. Offer/source **agreed-value** policies so the rider's true invested value is the payout — directly addresses the "my bike is worth more than its book value" objection.

Supporting plays (secondary):
- **Specialist-broker trust block done right:** the niche brokers omit licensing/years/ratings. We pair ADV specialization WITH visible licensing, carrier panel, and reviews — closing the credibility gap they leave open.
- **ADV-rider content brain:** ride-report-flavored guides (TAT prep, ADV gear coverage checklist, "is your GS underinsured", off-road-park rules by state). Carriers' knowledge bases are generic; no one owns *adventure* content depth.
- **Multi-bike / quiver discount messaging** aimed at riders who own a dual-sport + a big ADV tourer.
- **Roadside / recovery for remote breakdowns** — frame roadside around being stranded on a trail, not a freeway shoulder.
